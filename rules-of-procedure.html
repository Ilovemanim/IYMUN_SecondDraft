Rules of Procedure

Official Guidelines for IYMUN XIV

Overview

IYMUN follows the official United Nations Rules of Procedure. These rules ensure fair and orderly debate while providing delegates with an authentic multilateral diplomacy experience.

General Principles

  • Equality of Delegation: All countries represented have equal speaking rights
  • Respect and Decorum: Delegates must maintain respectful conduct at all times
  • Adherence to Agenda: Debate must remain focused on committee agenda items
  • Speaking Rights: Delegates speak only when recognized by the Chair
  • Diplomatic Immunity: Delegates are protected from disruptive conduct

Debate Procedures

Formal Debate

During formal debate sessions, delegates address the Chair in the English language. Speaking time for each delegate is limited, typically between 60-90 seconds. Delegates may deliver national positions, questions, or responses.

Moderated Caucus

Moderated caucuses allow for focused discussion on specific topics. Delegates must raise their placards to be recognized and speak on assigned topics. Speaking time is limited to 30-60 seconds per speaker.

Unmoderated Caucus

Delegates engage in informal discussions without Chair intervention. This is crucial for building coalitions, negotiating working papers, and drafting resolutions.

Resolution Process

  1. Introduction: Sponsors present the working paper to committee
  2. Debate: Committee debates the resolution substantively
  3. Amendments: Delegates may propose amendments to the resolution
  4. Voting: Committee votes on the resolution and amendments

Points and Motions

Point of Personal Privilege

To address a personal discomfort or interference with a delegate's ability to participate

Point of Order

To call attention to a violation of the Rules of Procedure

Motion to Move Into Unmoderated Caucus

To transition to informal discussion mode

Motion to Divide the Question

To split a resolution for separate voting on different provisions

Delegate Conduct Standards

  • Maintain professional and respectful language at all times
  • Avoid personal attacks or disrespectful comments
  • Represent your assigned country's position faithfully
  • Abstain from devices during formal debate sessions
  • Follow Chair instructions promptly
  • Maintain accurate position papers and research
