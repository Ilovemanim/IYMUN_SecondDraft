WHO Committee

World Health Organization

Committee Overview

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. As a WHO delegate, you will address critical health issues affecting global populations and develop collaborative solutions to promote health equity and disease prevention.

About WHO

The World Health Organization, established in 1948, is dedicated to directing and coordinating international efforts to achieve the highest possible level of health for all people. WHO works with governments and partners to address health emergencies, improve maternal and child health, combat infectious diseases, and promote healthy lifestyles.

Agenda Items for IYMUN

Pandemic Preparedness and Response

Developing global frameworks to improve preparedness for emerging infectious diseases and pandemics, including vaccine distribution mechanisms and early warning systems.

Mental Health Crisis in Youth

Addressing rising rates of depression, anxiety, and suicide among young people globally, and developing strategies to improve mental health services and support systems.

Committee Information

Committee Size: 20 delegates

Difficulty Level: Beginner - Intermediate
