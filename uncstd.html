UNCSTD Committee

Committee Overview

The UN Commission on Science and Technology for Development (UNCSTD) is a high-level intergovernmental body focused on promoting science and technology in service of development. As a delegate, you will work to bridge the digital divide, promote innovation for sustainable development, and ensure equitable access to technological advancements across the globe.

About UNCSTD

Established to address the relationship between science, technology, and economic development, UNCSTD advises the UN General Assembly on matters related to technology transfer, capacity building, and the integration of science and technology into development strategies. The Commission works to ensure that technological innovations benefit developing nations and contribute to the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals.

Agenda Items for UNCSTD

Digital Divide and Tech Inequality

Addressing disparities in access to technology and internet connectivity between developed and developing nations, with focus on solutions for rural and underserved communities.

Artificial Intelligence and Ethics

Developing international guidelines for responsible AI development and deployment, ensuring AI benefits are equitably distributed and ethical concerns are addressed.

Committee Information

Committee Size: 20 delegates

Difficulty Level: Beginner - Intermediate

Chair: Experienced MUN facilitators with technology and development expertise
